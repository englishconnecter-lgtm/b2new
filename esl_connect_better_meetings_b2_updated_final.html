How to Make Meetings Better

This B2 lesson helps students discuss why meetings often feel pointless and how to make them more useful, inclusive and productive.

Lead-in:
  1. What makes a meeting useful? What makes a meeting a waste of time?
  2. What is the most common problem you experience in meetings?
  3. Should every meeting have a strict time limit?
  4. How can a chair make sure quieter people are included?

Grammar: Advice and Recommendations

Use B2 structures to give advice about better meetings.

StructureUseExample
should / shouldn’t + verbgeneral adviceThe chair should send out an agenda.
make sure + clausestrong practical adviceMake sure everyone has a chance to speak.
It’s a good idea to + verbsoft recommendationIt’s a good idea to send notes afterwards.
You might want to + verbgentle suggestionYou might want to speak early in the meeting.
