Growth Mindset vs Fixed Mindset

B2 lesson on ability, feedback, failure, effort, obstacles and continual improvement.

Mindset and Personal Growth

This B2 lesson helps students understand Carol Dweck’s idea of fixed and growth mindsets and discuss how mindset affects learning, feedback and risk-taking.

Lead-in:
  1. Do you think talent is mostly natural, or can people improve almost any ability through practice?
  2. Do you think intelligence and talent are mostly fixed, or can they develop over time?
  3. How do you usually react when you make a mistake or fail at something important?
  4. What kinds of comments from teachers, managers or parents can help people build confidence and resilience?

Grammar: Contrast and Comparison

Use B2 structures to compare fixed and growth mindsets.

StructureUseExample
while / whereascontrast two ideasA fixed mindset avoids failure, whereas a growth mindset learns from it.
more likely to + verbdescribe probabilityGrowth-minded people are more likely to embrace challenges.
less likely to + verbdescribe lower probabilityFixed-minded people are less likely to take risks.
rather than + -ing / nouncontrast preference or focusThey focus on improvement rather than perfection.

Grammar Exercise 3: Sentence Transformations

A fixed mindset avoids failure. A growth mindset learns from failure.
Use “more likely to”.
People with a growth mindset usually embrace challenges.
Use “less likely to”.
People with a fixed mindset often avoid creative risks.
Use “rather than”.
A growth mindset focuses on improvement, not perfection.
