B2 lesson on charts, evidence, public health, social change, misinformation and climate communication.

Charts That Changed the World

This B2 lesson helps students understand how data visualisation can explain complex information, persuade people and sometimes mislead them.

Lead-in:
  1. What makes a chart powerful: accuracy, beauty, simplicity or emotional impact?
  2. Which type of chart do you find easiest to understand?
  3. Can a chart be accurate but still misleading? How?
  4. When have you seen a chart influence people’s opinions or decisions?

Grammar: Cause, Effect and Impact

Use B2 structures to explain how charts influence decisions and society.

StructureUseExample
led to + nounresultSnow’s map led to the pump being shut down.
helped to + verbpositive contributionNightingale’s chart helped to improve hospitals.
was used to + verbpurpose in the passiveThe Kallikak chart was used to support eugenics.
by showing + noun phrasemethodDu Bois challenged stereotypes by showing achievements.
