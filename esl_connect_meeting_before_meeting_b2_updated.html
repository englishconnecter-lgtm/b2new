The Meeting Before the Meeting

B2 lesson on workplace influence, decision-making, proposals, informal conversations and unspoken rules.

Open video on YouTube

Unspoken Workplace Rules

This B2 lesson helps students understand how decisions are often influenced before the official meeting begins.

Lead-in:
  1. Have you ever been in a meeting where the real decision seemed to have been made before the meeting started?
  2. Is it smart or unfair to discuss an idea privately with colleagues before presenting it formally?
  3. Why might people prefer to test an idea informally before a formal meeting?
  4. How can junior employees influence decisions without formal authority?
  5. What is the difference between building support and manipulating people?

Role Play: Socializing a Proposal

Practise preparing an idea before an important meeting.

Situation: One student has a proposal for improving the company. The other student is a colleague who gives feedback before the official meeting.

Student A: Proposal Owner

Present your idea informally and ask for feedback, advice and possible objections.

Student B: Colleague / Naysayer

Ask questions, give honest feedback and suggest who else Student A should speak to.
