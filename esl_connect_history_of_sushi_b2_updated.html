The Journey of Sushi

This B2 lesson helps students understand how sushi developed from a method of preserving fish into a global food trend.

Lead-in:
  1. Do you like sushi? Why do you think it became so popular around the world?
  2. How do traditional foods change when they become popular in another country?
  3. Would you be willing to try a food that can be dangerous if it is prepared incorrectly?
  4. What food from your country has become popular internationally?

Grammar: Describing Historical Change

Use B2 structures to explain how something developed over time.

StructureUseExample
used to + verbpast habit or past stateSushi used to be a way to preserve fish.
was / were + past participlepassive voice for historyRaw fish was added to rice in the 1820s.
by + time expressionchange completed before a timeBy the 15th century, people were eating the rice.
as + clausetwo developments happening togetherAs sushi spread, new versions appeared.

Grammar Exercise 3: Sentence Transformations

Use passive voice.
People packed fish with salt and rice.
Use “By the 15th century”.
People started eating the rice before the 15th century ended.
Use “As sushi spread”.
New Westernised versions appeared when sushi became more international.
